Ovarian cysts are a prevalent health concern in women of all ages. While most cysts are harmless and go unnoticed, others may cause symptoms that necessitate treatment, such as surgery. Understanding Ovarian Cysts

Ovarian cysts are fluid-filled sacs that form on or inside the ovaries. Most of them are begin and disappear spontaneously without any treatment. Some of them grow big enough to cause pain and discomfort, or even complications such as torsion or rupturing, requiring a surgical intervention.

Types of Ovarian Cysts

Functional cysts :- The most prevalent form, are associated with the menstrual cycle.

  1. Follicular cysts form when an egg-containing follicle fails to burst and release during ovulation.
  2. Corpus Luteum Cysts form when a follicle releases an egg but fails to shrink, allowing fluid to collect.

Non-Functional cysts :-These are not associated with the menstrual cycle and may include:

  1. Dermoid cysts include a variety of tissues, including hair, skin, and teeth.
  2. Cystadenomas are benign growths on the ovary’s surface that may be filled with fluid or mucus.

Endometriomas arise from endometriosis, a disorder in which tissue comparable to the uterine lining grows outside the uterus.

When is Surgery Required for Ovarian Cysts ?

Indications for surgical intervention include:
  • The cyst exceeds 5 cm
  • Severe pain or hemorrhage can cause further complications.
  • There are apprehensions of malignancy.
  • It leads to complications such as ovarian torsion.

Diagnosis through Procedures for Ovarian Cysts

Before suggesting the surgical treatment, a doctor runs a number of tests, among which are:

  • Pelvic Ultrasound: Visualizes the cyst’s size, shape, and type.
  • Blood Tests: To rule out cancer, CA-125 blood test may be advised.
  • MRI/CT Scans: Detailed assessment for complex cases.

Dr. Divya Kumar employs the best modern diagnostic methodology to bring forth an accurate diagnosis which then enables her to put down the best treatment modalities.

Types of Ovarian Cyst Surgery

The different surgical procedures are based on the size of the cyst, the type of cyst, and the symptoms presented:

Laparoscopic Surgery (Minimally Invasive):
  1. Used for smaller benign cysts.
  2. Involves making several small cuts in which a camera and instruments are inserted to extract the cyst.
  3. Benefits include Less pain, less scarring, quick recovery.
Laparotomy (Open surgery):
  1. Used for bigger cysts or if malignancy is suspected.
  2. Is marked by a wide incision for improved access and inspection.

Dr. Divya Kumar is well-versed in laparoscopic methods that promise less pain and quicker recovery to patients. Her strong interest in minimally invasive surgery has made her a popular option for ovarian cyst surgery in Faridabad.

Pre-Surgical Preparation

Preparation plays a key role in the success of ovarian cyst removal:

  • Medical Evaluation: Imaging and blood tests are performed in a determination of overall health.
  • Fasting: Most commonly necessitating admission 8-12 hours prior to operation.
  • Medications: Advice regarding current drugs and allergies must be given.

Post-Operative Care and Recovery

Different surgeries have different recovery patterns:

  1. Discharge from laparoscopic surgery is normally on the same day or within a day, and post-operative recuperation consists of 1 to 2 weeks of relaxation.
  2. Patients undergoing laparotomy require hospitalization for 2 to 4 days and a recovery period of 4 to 6 weeks.
Tips for a Smooth Recovery:
  1. Please avoid heavy lifting or strenuous exercises for a couple of weeks.
  2. Maintain a healthy diet for healing.
  3. Maintain follow-ups with the doctor to check recovery.
