High-Risk Pregnancy: What You Need to Know

Understanding High-Risk Pregnancy

A high-risk pregnancy refers to a pregnancy where the mother or baby faces an increased risk of complications before, during, or after childbirth. Various factors contribute to the classification of a pregnancy as high-risk, including maternal age, pre-existing medical conditions such as diabetes or hypertension, multiple pregnancies (twins, triplets), genetic factors, lifestyle choices such as smoking or substance abuse, and previous pregnancy complications.
